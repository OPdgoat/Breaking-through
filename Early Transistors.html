The First Transistors

https://www.lens.org/lens/patent/147-350-838-059-788

Field effect and Point contact

The first two people who worked towards solid state information transfer were Julius Lilienfeld and Oskar Heil. Lilienfeld produced two patents relating to transistors, the more famous one talks about a three-electrode structure on a copper semiconductor. This device is close to a modern field effect transistor, however there is no documentation of it ever being produced or used. Oskar Heil studied silicon crystals in an attempt to increase purity, but a cracked crystal had an odd behavior where the current flowing through changed over water and over a hot soldering iron. This was due to each side of the crystal containing different purities and was used as the first P-N Junction.

The first transistor, invented by American physicists John Bardeen, Walter H. Brattain, and William B. Shockley, https://www.britannica.com/technology/transistor/Innovation-at-Bell-Labs

The majority of early pioneering efforts in transistors happened at the famous Bell Labs. In the span of a single month John Bardeen and Walter Brattain managed to work together and produce the first point-contact transistor. The original prototype was made from silicon, but it was discovered that germanium greatly increased amplification.
