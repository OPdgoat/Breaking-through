Important Definitions

Triode – a vacuum tube having three electrodes

Electrode – A conductor through which electricity enters or leaves an object

Anode – Positively charged electrode by which the electrons leave a device

Cathode – Negatively charged electrode by which the electrons enter a device


Synopsis

Transistors broke through the barrier of capacity from thermionic triodes with a solid-state alternative. They improved vastly when they started using silicon as it allowed for mass production with the most used silicon transistor being the metal-oxide-semiconductor field-effect transistor. They opened the doors of possibility for electronics to become vastly more powerful than vacuum tubes ever allowed, without transistors the modern world couldn’t exist as they are included in every electronic device made since the 1940s.
